Overview

This is the Overview of Rugby:

Rugby is a sport made for hooligans but its played by men. You don't need a lot of equipment, just a pair of cleats, a mouthguard, and a jersey and some shorts and a ball. It can be played by anyone, men, women, and children.It is a very inclusive sport. The game is mostly played by the nations of the British Commonwealth. But other countries around the world play it. This sport teaches respect, camaraderie and other character building traits. There are two from of rugby. The first is called Rugby Union and the other is Rugby League. These two iterations of the sport are very similar but have different rules. On this site we will explore Rugby Union. Links for both versions will be provided below.

Rules

The rules of Rugby:

  • Only 15 players on the field.
  • No tackels above the neck.
  • Don't pass the ball forward.
  • Respect the referee.
  • Respect your opponent.
  • The ball has to be touched down in the endzone inorder to count as a try.
  • If a ruck is formed then players can't enter from the side.
  • If a maul is formed then players can't join from the side.

History

This is the History of Rugby:

  • 1823: A young 16 year old student named William Webb Ellis at the Rugby School decided to pick up the ball in a game of soccer.
  • 1871: The Rugby Football Union is formed.
  • 1871: The first International game is played between England and Scotland.
  • 1895: Rugby football splits into Rugby Union and Rugby League.
  • 1900: Rugby Union is featured in the 1900 and 1924 Olympics.
  • 1930: Rugby union's European Cup starts, outside the Five Nations. It is interrupted by WWII.
  • 1952: Rugby union's European Cup restarts.
  • 1956: Springboks' rugby union tour of New Zealand. South Africa suffer their first ever test series loss against New Zealand.
